Environmental Impact

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Effects

Methane and Carbon dioxide particularly generated by FLW contributing to the greenhouse gases. When food waste ends up in the landfill, it decomposes anaerobically (without oxygen) that produces methane, which is a potent greenhouse gas.

Biodiversity and Pollution

Effects

Food waste that is allowed to decompose in landfills pollutes groundwater and streams, damages land biodiversity, and releases greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. Pollutants are released throughout the decomposition process, which can contaminate ecosystems nearby.

Carbon Footprint

Effects

Different types of food have varying levels of carbon footprint. The main contributors to the carbon footprint of food waste are cereals (34%), meat (21%), and vegetables (21%). Emissions are caused by diesel used in the manufacture of grains and nitrogen fertilizers.

Economic Impact

Main Contributors to Economic Cost

Effects

The primary sources of the economic cost of FLW are fruits (19%), vegetables (23%), meat (21%), and cereals (18%). Because of its high production cost per kilogram, meat contributes disproportionately to food waste even though it makes up just 4% of the total food wasted (FAO, 2013b, 55).

Effects
