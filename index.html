What is Food Waste

Food waste is a specific piece of food loss, which the US Department of Agriculture’s (USDA) Economic Research Service (ERS), defines as “food discarded by retailers due to color or appearance and plate waste by consumers. ”Food waste includes the half-eaten meal left on the plate at a restaurant, food scraps from preparing a meal at home and the sour milk a family pours down the drain.

Cause

Food waste stems from overproduction, consumer habits, and supply chain inefficiencies, leading to the unnecessary disposal of edible food.

Impact

Food waste has significant environmental, economic, and social repercussions, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions, resource loss, and food insecurity.

Solution

Strategies to address food waste include improving distribution systems, educating consumers on storage and meal planning, implementing policies for surplus food redistribution, and promoting composting and food recovery programs.

What is Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that all people enjoy peace and prosperity by 2030. Adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, the SDGs encompass 17 interconnected goals addressing a broad range of social, economic, and environmental challenges.

Food Sustainability and Security.

SDGs 2, 12, 3, 13, and 15 collectively address the complex nexus of food sustainability, waste reduction, and health. By tackling food waste, promoting responsible consumption, ensuring access to nutritious food, mitigating climate impacts, and fostering sustainable land use, these goals synergize efforts towards ending hunger, promoting well-being, and safeguarding ecosystems.
